1st & Island

The 1st & Island project is a 36-storey high-rise residential development in San Diego, California, featuring 212 residential units with three levels of below-grade and above-grade parking. The HVAC system is designed around a high-efficiency Variable Refrigerant Volume (VRV) system, with outdoor condensing units located on the roof and level 5. Horizontal VRF fan coils serve the suites, and vertical insulated refrigerant piping runs through shafts to distribute heating and cooling efficiently. Residential unit ventilation is provided via intake air ducts, supplying fresh air directly to fan coils, while a make-up air unit delivers 25 CFM per door to corridors. The parking garage ventilation is controlled by a CO monitoring system, ensuring 0.75 CFM/ft² ventilation rate with VFD-driven exhaust fans for energy efficiency.

A dedicated smoke control strategy includes stair pressurization, corridor ventilation, and a fire control room air supply for emergency conditions. The building automation system (BAS) integrates HVAC controls, allowing for remote monitoring and optimization of ventilation, heating, and cooling loads. Energy efficiency measures include the use of ECM variable-speed motors in fan coils and common areas, reducing power consumption. Amenity spaces and utility rooms are ventilated per ASHRAE standards, ensuring proper airflow and air quality. As a Mechanical Designer, I contributed to the HVAC system layout, equipment selection, and load calculations, ensuring compliance with California’s Title 24 energy codes. The design emphasizes sustainability, efficiency, and occupant comfort, making it a state-of-the-art high-rise HVAC system.
